Expert Panel · Phase 2

Judge Registration

Your evaluations contribute to the Content Validity Index (CVI) calculation for the Hofstede Individual Instrument.

About this instrument: The Cultural Awareness instrument is an original psychometric tool designed to operationalize Hofstede's six cultural value dimensions at the individual level. It is currently in Phase 1 (Item Design) and requires expert panel validation (Phase 2) before field deployment. The instrument is part of the research framework Predicting Organizational Communication Failure: The VICAR Model (2026), and will serve as the primary data collection tool for a Southeast Asian low-cost carrier (SALCC) field study. It is designed for independent publication upon completion of full psychometric validation. Theoretical foundations: Hofstede (2001); Taras et al. (2016); Beck & Cowan (1996).
Evaluation criteria (1–4 per item): (1) Relevance to Hofstede dimension · (2) Clarity of wording · (3) Cultural sensitivity · (4) Social desirability risk. Ratings ≥ 3 count toward I-CVI. Decision thresholds: I-CVI ≥ 0.78 retain · 0.60–0.77 revise · <0.60 eliminate. S-CVI/Ave ≥ 0.90 required (Lynn, 1986; Polit & Beck, 2006).
